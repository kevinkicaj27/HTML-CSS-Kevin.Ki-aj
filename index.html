Biography
From Europe to NBA

Luka Doncic (born February 28, 1999) is a Slovenian professional basketball player for the Dallas Mavericks of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He also represents the Slovenian national team. Born in Ljubljana, Doncic shone as a youth player for Union Olimpija before joining the youth academy of Real Madrid. In 2015 he made his debut for the academy's senior team at age 16, becoming the youngest in club history. In 2018, Doncic declared for the NBA draft, joining the Dallas Mavericks.

Awards

NBA Rookie of the Year

After his first year in NBA, Luka Doncic was selected as NBA Rookie of the Year. This award is given to the best rookie of the regular season.

3× NBA All-Star

During the interval 2020-2022, Luka Doncic was selected three times to play in NBA All-Stars match.

3× All-NBA First Team

Luka Doncic was selected three times as part of All-NBA First Team, as the best point guard of each season.

EuroLeague champion

In 2018, Luka Doncic won the EuroLeague as part of Real Madrid. He was also named EuroLeague MVP.

3× Liga ACB

During 2015, 2016 and 2018 years, Luka Doncic won the Liga ACB as part of Real Madrid. In 2018, he was also named Liga ACB MVP.

EuroBasket champion

During EuroBasket held in Turkey in 2017, Luka Doncic represented Slovenia and helped his country to win the gold medal.

Career

Union Olimpija

  • Luka joined Union Olimpija when he was only 8 years old.
  • He played for u-14 team.
  • Representing Olimpija at the u-14 Vasas Intesa Sanpaolo Cup, Dončić was named the MVP.

Real Madrid

  • At 13 years of age, Dončić signed a five-year contract with Real Madrid.
  • He won different trophies for Real Madrid.
  • Luka won also two MVP awards (Liga ACB & EuroLeague).

National Career
A new star for Europe

On September 22, 2016, Doncic announced that he would represent the senior men's Slovenian national team for the rest of his career. Doncic was a Slovenian squad member for EuroBasket 2017, where his country won its first gold medal after going undefeated (9–0) in the tournament.
